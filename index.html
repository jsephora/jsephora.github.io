About me

Mark Twain is (wrongly) given credit for saying that the coldest winter he ever spent was a summer in San Francisco. Well I guess he's never spent a winter in Canberra.

I began my career in San Francisco, late in the summer of '99, during what we now refer to as the "dotcom bubble". The city was at the heart of the dotcom boom and the parties were extravagant! I count myself lucky to have graduated university just in time to get a taste of it, but it was shortlived and I found myself competing with hundreds of out of work web designers most of whom had far better credentials than me.

During that post-bubble period I had all kinds of jobs, met all kinds of people and partied like it was still 1999. It was a fantastic learning experience.

I've never stopped learning. For me, every spare minute is a chance to learn something new or improve my skills.

Recently I've also started passing on some of my knowledge and helping the next generation of web designers to gain new (and old) tricks.

ATO Beta project

Website design and development for the ATO

ATO's "Beta" site evolved from being a simple blog to an online testing hub for the ATO. On this site, the ATO can publish new concepts, website improvements and prototypes. The public can then test them and provide feedback, at their own convenience.

This was an Agile scrum project and I was responsible for design and front-end development for the build stage. Now I have a multitude of roles, including creating prototypes, conducting user testing, marketing and compiling and analysing test results.
